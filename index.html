Who are the big pandas?

The big panda, or bamboo bear, is a mammal belonging to the family of bears. Native to central China, large pandas are easily recognizable by their black and white coat color.

Despite its taxonomic classification as a predator, the panda feeds mainly on bamboo ‚Äî up to 99% of its diet consists of bamboo shoots and roots.

Panda lifestyle

Pandas lead a predominantly solitary lifestyle. Each adult panda has its own clearly defined territory, which it protects from other pandas.

Large pandas can spend up to 14 hours a day eating, consuming up to 38 kg of bamboo daily. The rest of the time they mostly rest and sleep.

Panda conservation and protection

The Great Panda has long been endangered, but thanks to conservation efforts, its status was changed to "vulnerable" in 2016.

The Chinese government has established more than 50 panda sanctuaries that protect about 67% of the large panda population in the wild and more than 50% of their habitat.
